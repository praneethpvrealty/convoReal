## 1. City Rollout Strategy — Two Waves

Running four cities on Day 1 splits your white-glove onboarding four ways exactly when
hand-holding matters most. Instead:

| Wave | Cities | Days | Why this order |
|------|--------|------|----------------|
| **Wave 1 (invite-only)** | Hyderabad, Bangalore | 1–45 | Dense channel-partner networks, high WhatsApp adoption, lower CAC, easier association access. Manufacture your 20 case studies here. |
| **Wave 2 (public launch)** | Mumbai, Gurgaon | 45–90 | India's two most expensive, most competitive broker markets. Enter them *with proof in hand*, not cold. Mumbai/Gurgaon agents respond to "300 agents in Hyderabad already use this," not to promises. |

All four cities see the waitlist site from Day 1 — Mumbai and Gurgaon agents can join the
waitlist immediately; they just onboard in Wave 2. Scarcity works *for* you there.

### Per-City Cheat Sheet

**Content language strategy: three tracks — English, Kannada, Hindi.**
English is the pan-city B2B layer (LinkedIn, landing page, YouTube integrations). Hindi
covers Gurgaon and Mumbai natively and works as the second language in Hyderabad's broker
community. Kannada is exclusively for Bangalore, where it buys outsized goodwill. Telugu
and Marathi versions can be added later if Hyderabad/Mumbai conversion lags — the copy
structure below makes that a translation job, not a rewrite.

| | Micro-markets to seed first | Community entry points | Content languages | Launch angle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hyderabad** | Kokapet, Tellapur, Narsingi, Kompally (heavy primary/pre-launch activity) | NAR-India chapter, developer channel-partner (CP) networks, TS RERA agent list | English + Hindi | "Pre-launch inventory moves on WhatsApp. Now your whole pipeline does." |
| **Bangalore** | Whitefield, Sarjapur Road, North Bangalore/Devanahalli | NAR Bangalore chapter, CP networks of mid-size developers, K-RERA agent list | English + Kannada | Tech-city angle: "The sales OS built the way you already work — on WhatsApp." |
| **Mumbai** | Thane, Chembur/Wadala, Andheri–Goregaon belt | Estate Agents Association of India (EAAI, HQ Mumbai), MahaRERA agent register, CP networks | Hindi + English | Speed angle: "In Mumbai the first agent to reply wins. Reply in seconds, from every channel." |
| **Gurgaon** | Golf Course Ext. Road, Dwarka Expressway, New Gurgaon | Broker federations/NAR chapter, HRERA Gurugram agent list, big CP ecosystems (DLF/M3M belt) | Hindi + English | Status angle: "The Founding Agent badge your clients will see." |

> **RERA agent registers are your outbound goldmine.** All four states publish registered
> agent lists — that is a legal, public, pre-qualified lead database for your SDR/founder
> outreach. Start there before spending a rupee on ads.

| 1 | WhatsApp broker groups (organic) | Founding Agents forward the launch; you never post cold into groups yourself. |
| 2 | YouTube realtor-educator channels | 5–8 deep integrations (10-min "how I run my day on ConvoReal") — mix Hindi and Kannada creators with 1–2 English pan-India channels. |

| 4 | On-ground | 1 association event per city per month; sign up + import contacts on the spot. |
| 5 | Google search ads | Only on high-intent terms: "whatsapp crm real estate", "real estate lead management app". Small budget, always-on. |

## 8. The One Metric

**Time-to-first-broadcast.** An agent who imports inventory and sends a broadcast in week 1
stays and refers. Review it weekly per city; every other number follows this one.
